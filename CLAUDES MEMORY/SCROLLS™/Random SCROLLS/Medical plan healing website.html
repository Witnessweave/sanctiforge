1) Quick Start — Today

Fasting Window

  • Start 16:8 (fast 16h, eat in 8h).
  • Consider 18:6 later; OMAD short-term if appropriate.
  • Hydrate; black coffee/unsweet tea during fast if desired.

Food — Low Carb, God-grown

  • Remove sugars, white flour, soda, sweets, most boxed snacks.
  • Base plates on non‑starchy veggies, protein, healthy fats.
  • Target < 50g net carbs/day initially.

Walk After Meals

  • Walk 10–20 min within 30 min after eating.
  • This blunts post‑meal glucose spikes.

Sleep

  • Aim for 7–9 hours nightly.
  • Poor sleep → higher glucose/insulin resistance.

2) Natural Remedy Stack

Safety First: If you use glucose‑lowering meds (e.g., metformin, sulfonylureas, insulin), these remedies can further lower sugars. Coordinate with a clinician to avoid hypoglycemia.

Berberine

  • 500 mg, 2–3×/day before meals.
  • Supports insulin sensitivity; GI upset possible—start lower.

Cinnamon (Ceylon)

  • ~1 tsp/day in water/tea or food.
  • May reduce fasting glucose and slow carb absorption.

Gymnema sylvestre

  • Capsule/tea ~30 min before meals.
  • Can reduce sugar cravings and absorption.

Bitter melon

  • Tea, tincture, or cooked fruit as tolerated.
  • Insulin‑like effect; monitor for low glucose.

Apple Cider Vinegar

  • 1 Tbsp in water ~15 min before meals.
  • Blunts post‑meal glucose; protect tooth enamel (use straw, rinse).

Fenugreek or Holy Basil (Tulsi)

  • Fenugreek: soak seeds overnight; drink water in AM.
  • Tulsi: tea/capsule for stress + glucose balance.

4) Tracking & Targets

Daily

  • Fasting glucose on waking (target: < 100 mg/dL over time).
  • Optional: 1‑hr and 2‑hr post‑meal checks during learning phase.

Every 3 Months

  • A1C (remission target: < 5.7% without meds).
  • Weight, waist circumference, sleep quality.

5) Meds & Safety

Coordinate with a clinician. Fasting, low‑carb eating, and the herb stack can markedly lower glucose. If you’re on insulin or sulfonylureas, discuss dose adjustments to prevent hypoglycemia.
  • Who should avoid unsupervised fasting? Pregnancy/breastfeeding, underweight, eating disorders, brittle diabetes, certain meds—get medical guidance.
  • Interactions: Berberine may interact with some meds; cinnamon (Ceylon) preferred due to lower coumarin; bitter melon + meds may compound effects.
This material is for education and discipleship, not a substitute for personalized medical care.
