Why the Sunflower?

The sunflower follows the light. Even when the sun moves, the flower shifts its face to meet it. That’s called heliotropism. It’s one of the most poetic and powerful biological responses in the natural world — and it speaks volumes about what kind of person you are, or what kind of man you could become in Christ.

This flower wasn’t chosen because it’s popular or beautiful (even though it is). It was chosen because it represents direction, resilience, and consistency. It represents strength, yet softness. It stands tall, but never turns away from the light.

In a world where everything wants you to cave in, sit down, or give up — this flower stands. And it stands on purpose.
