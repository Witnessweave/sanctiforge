From Swap Meet Hustle to Streetwear Brand

A short testimony written as a mini-scroll. Tap highlighted words to reveal deeper context without leaving the page.

My name is Abel Arreola, and Threads On Fire started with nothing but a vision, a stack of tees, and a grind mentality forged from real struggle.

I grew up in a low-income Mexican-American home where nothing was handed out — everything was earned. Back in 2012, I hit rock bottom. I had just lost my job. My car was gone. No savings. No plan. But I had one thing left — fire. That inner fire to create something bigger than my situation.

So I hit the local swap meet.

Every weekend, I’d wake up early, set up my table in the hot sun, and hustle. I started by flipping second-hand gear with my own twist. I wasn’t chasing trends — I was building a vibe. Matching fits with clean sneakers, studying culture, understanding the language of the streets. My tent grew into a booth, and the booth became a movement because people saw the vision before the logo ever existed.

The Spark Behind the Brand

By 2015, I leveled up. I launched my first website and started shipping gear coast to coast. What started under a pop-up tent became a growing streetwear movement — backed by style, substance, and heritage. I rebranded as Threads On Fire — a name that speaks to the passion that kept me pushing when everything else burned down.

Today, Threads On Fire is more than a clothing brand — it’s a reflection of where I come from, who I represent, and where we’re headed. We don’t sell hype. We build confidence through clean, sneaker-matching streetwear that stands on identity, not clout. We stand against clout chasing and for legacy.

If you’re reading this, you’ve felt the fire too. We don’t sell heat. We pass the torch. Pass the torch and walk with us.

Swap Meet

This wasn’t just a place to sell. It was my open-air classroom. Every table taught me humility, every customer taught me hustle. I saw real stories in the dust, heat, and handshakes. My grind was built here—out in the open, nothing to hide, everything to prove.

“Faith doesn’t need a fancy start. Just a tent, a table, and a reason to get up again.”

Rock Bottom

Rock bottom isn’t a place. It’s a season where all you have left is honesty and hope. In my lowest moment, I realized the only way forward was up. Every scar, every doubt, became fuel for the fire.

Biblical fire: Joseph in prison. David in the cave. Every chosen one finds fire when there’s nowhere left to run but God.

Fire
